Transformations in Noether's Theorem

In Noether's theorem, we consider a transformation of coordinates described by: $$t' = t + \epsilon \tau(q_k(t), t)$$ $$q_i'(t') = q_i(t) + \epsilon \eta_i(q_k(t), t)$$

There is a nice geometrical way to think about this. Let us consider the configuration space with coordinate axes $q_1, \dots, q_N, t$. The evolution of our system may be described by a trajectory (line) in configuration space. Consider the vector field in configuration space given by: $$\vec{X} = (\eta_1(q_k(t), t), \dots, \eta_N(q_k(t), t), \tau(q_k(t), t))$$ Now, imagine that we displace each point on our original trajectory by a small amount $\epsilon \vec{X}$. This produces a new trajectory in configuration space that is described by $q_1'(t'), \dots, q_N'(t')$.

Note that in our original equations, $q_i'(t')$ is defined in terms of $t$, but its argument is $t'$. The reason is that after displacement, the point $(q_1', \dots, q_N')$ is now associated with the time $t'$, since that is where the $t$ associated with the original $(q_1, \dots, q_N)$ was displaced to.
